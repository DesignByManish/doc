Noel Leeming fulfilment estimate

Noel Leeming (part of The Warehouse Group) is a leading tech retailer in New Zealand. When I joined, the Noel Leeming contact center was receiving a lot of customer calls asking about delivery and collection timeframes and store stock information before or after they purchased a product online.

To improve the customer experience, we started unpacking the problem with the objective of –

How might we improve online communication about delivery, click & collection and store stock estimate so that our users feel confident about when they’ll receive their product?

Design process

As a lead UX on this initiative, I worked through the e2e design process for this project including discovery, ideation, prototyping, user testing, UI design and working with remotely located product team to implement the final UI within agile delivery framework. This was the first time the team & stakeholders were working closely with a dedicated UX designer and focussing on customer needs rather than business or tech requirements. So this was also a great opportunity to coach the business and tech teams on customer-centric problem solving and design processes, and take them on the design journey as we progressed on the project.

Discovery

We reviewed the current state and identified key issues leading to this problem.

Unreliable estimates

Incorrect, confusing or no delivery estimates, especially around special orders which make up for a substantial % of total products.

Lack of contextual grouping

Fulfilment options scattered across the product details page make them difficult to discover and read.

Possible poor placement

The ‘find in store’ button had only 6% click through rate, out of which only 0.05% users ended up completing the transaction.

Inconsistent messaging throughout the journey

Messaging about delivery & collection was inconsistent across purchase & post-purchase journey.

Prototype & test

We did 2 rounds of prototype & testing on high fidelity prototypes to understand users’ mental model around fulfillment options, and what information are they looking for to make the right decision throughout the online purchase journey.

User testing

Test insights

  • Most users prefer estimated dates over estimated days.
  • None of the users noticed store specific click & collect estimate.
  • Most users want to see stock availability in their preferred store first.

Design & deliver

After collecting insights from the multiple rounds of testing we arrived at the final solution. Before implementing the final solution, we documented 13 different scenarios around product type and fulfilment rules, so we could design and have a message structure which is consistent across all the scenarios. The final solution had –

  • Grouped and simplified way of displaying fulfilment options.
  • Consistent and clear messaging about estimates across key scenarios and purchase journey.
  • Easier to understand dates, instead of days.

Results

After delivering the solution, and enhancing the experience we successfully managed to reduce customer complaints and calls around delivery, collection and store stock estimates. We also received customer feedback of the experience being much simpler with easier to understand fulfillment options and estimates which helped them in making their purchase decision. Through this initiative I was successful in demonstrating to the business stakeholders the importance of putting customer needs before business and tech needs, by following a customer-centric way of working.
