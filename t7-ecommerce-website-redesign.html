Torpedo7 online store redesign

Torpedo7 is a popular New Zealand retail store, for all adventure gear needs across indoor and outdoor sports and activities. Adventure enthusiasts and experts can shop popular adventure brands here, alongside Torpedo7 brand products, either online or from the various stores across New Zealand. We were tasked with the goal to create a market leading digital storefront and community â€“ the best for outdoor adventure.

My role

As a lead UX on this project, I worked with the head of design, business stakeholders, product owner and UI designer to set a vision for the UX and redesigning the eCommerce website. I worked on problem discovery, creating user flows, identifying JTBD (jobs to be done) and creating responsive wireframes. My responsibilities also included working with project manager on project timelines, doing sprint planning, gathering requirements, collaborating and managing expectations with business and other stakeholders, and facilitating sprint rituals.

Identifying problems

We started with understanding current user pain points through user testing the old website and doing a design review on that experience.

Not mobile friendly

The biggest problem we identified was having a poor and non-responsive mobile shopping experience. Even though a huge amount of users visit the mobile website, the conversion rate was poor. It was clear we had to take a mobile-first design approach.

No representation of adventure

Another problem was the perception of Torpedo7 as just a deal website. Our goal was to bring forward the â€˜experienceâ€™ part of any adventure and share the story with our users.

Poor visibility of range

There was also no visibility of the wide range of popular outdoor brands and products available on the website.

Process and planning

We followed the design process of discover, ideate, prototype & test, implement and measure. As we were a newly formed design team, it was really interesting and a great learning experience as we experimented with a few different techniques to identify the best ways to collaborate with each other.

Keeping in mind the fixed budget and timelines for the redesign project, planning was crucial to achieve our goals. We created a sprint based project plan, with a goal to deliver one part of core eCommerce flow at the end of every 2-week sprint. Planning in sprints helped us in having a lean approach which we could iterate on if needed (considering dependencies & blockers, if any) as we move ahead in the project.

Discovery

We started every sprint with a session with the business stakeholders and product manager to document business and user requirements around a part of the core flow. The outcome of the session was to set the sprint & UX goal after identifying the various JTBD (jobs to be done) for that flow. As part of the discovery we also looked at benchmarks and latest customer feedback from Hotjar, to help us set a direction for the rest of the sprint.

Prototype and test

Based on the sprint goals, we either did usability testing of part of the online purchase flow or on specific pages.

PDP price lock up test

The goal for this test was to identify which price lockup is easier for users to know the product price. We tested with 2 groups of people, where participants in each group were shown one of the options. One group was tasked with finding the product price, and we asked the other group the first 5 things they noticed on the screen.

Results

As fun as it was working on the actual project, looking at the results and the feedback from customers & business was extremely rewarding and exciting. Just a few months after the launch, we saw an increase in the mobile conversion rate from 0.52% to 0.75%. Moving forward, this also created a great space for the team to keep optimising the experience and continue improving on the results.
